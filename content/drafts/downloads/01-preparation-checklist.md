# Inside Line — Preparation Checklist

**Knowledge. Rights. Action.**

Use this checklist to see what you have, what you still need, and whether you are ready to take the next step. Checking a box does not mean you qualify for any legal remedy — it means you are getting organized.

---

**Disclaimer:** This worksheet is for educational purposes only and is not legal advice. Every case is different. Consult a qualified attorney when possible.

## Understand the Process (Research)

- [ ] I know what sentence modification is — and what it is not (not a new trial, not an appeal)
- [ ] I have read about basic eligibility factors for my situation
- [ ] I know whether I have filed a prior sentence modification
- [ ] I know whether I have filed an appeal
- [ ] I understand the difference between sentence modification and habeas corpus (if exploring both)
- [ ] I have reviewed Connecticut court process basics
- [ ] I know where my motion would be filed

---

## Documents to Gather

- [ ] Sentencing order / judgment of conviction
- [ ] Information (charging document) or police report summary
- [ ] Prior motions, appeals, or post-conviction filings
- [ ] Program completion certificates
- [ ] Educational transcripts or GED documentation
- [ ] Vocational training records
- [ ] Employment records (inside or pre-incarceration)
- [ ] Treatment or counseling completion records
- [ ] Institutional conduct / disciplinary history (accurate copy)
- [ ] Character reference letters
- [ ] Medical documentation (if relevant to my request)
- [ ] Victim-related correspondence (if applicable to my case)
- [ ] Reentry plan draft (housing, employment, support)

---

## Organization

- [ ] I have a dedicated folder or binder for my case
- [ ] Documents are sorted into labeled sections (not loose papers)
- [ ] I have a written timeline of key events (see Timeline Template)
- [ ] I have a list of what each document proves
- [ ] Copies are legible (not cut off or blurry)
- [ ] Pages are numbered or tabbed in order

---

## Before Filing

- [ ] I have drafted a clear personal statement (facts + supporting evidence)
- [ ] I have double-checked names, dates, and case numbers on every page
- [ ] I have identified required forms for my court
- [ ] I understand service / mailing requirements for the prosecutor
- [ ] I have allowed time to review everything — not rushing at a deadline
- [ ] I have considered whether my situation needs professional legal help
